A study in form, balance, and bold reduction.

This Bauhaus-inspired owl distills the subject down to its essential geometry -
circles, arcs, and color blocks arranged with intention. The symmetry is almost
mechanical, but the oversized eyes and softened edges keep it firmly in the
realm of character.

Primary colors anchor the composition against a deep black field, giving it
that classic Bauhaus tension: function versus expression, system versus
personality. The eyes pull you in immediately - equal parts alert, curious, and
slightly surreal.

Tufted with thick, high-pile texture, the rigid geometry breaks just enough at
the edges to remind you this is still fiber, still handmade. Precision meets
softness.

Bowlhaus sits somewhere between mascot and modernist study - a minimal owl that
feels both engineered and alive.
